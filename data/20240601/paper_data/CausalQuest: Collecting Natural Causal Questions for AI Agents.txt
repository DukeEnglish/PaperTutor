4.1 CausalTaxonomyDesign
Identifying Causal vs. Non-Causal Questions We build upon and expand Bondarenko et al.
(2022)’s definition of a causal question, which relies on causal chains (Schank, 1975). A causal
question requires the use of cause-and-effect knowledge or reasoning at least once in the causal
chaintoprovideananswer. Here,acausalchainisdefinedasasequenceofeventslinkedbycausal
dependencies: aneventcanenable,resultin,bethereasonof,cause,orleadtoanotherevent. We
expandthedefinitionusedinBondarenkoetal.(2022),byincludingthethreepossiblemechanistic
naturesofcausalquestionstobeapartofthedefinitionitself:
(a) Giventhecause,predicttheeffect(s): questionsseekingtounderstandtheimpactorout-
comeofaspecificcause,whichmightinvolvepredictionsaboutthefutureorhypothetical
scenarios,e.g.,WhatwouldhappeniftheUSAsuddenlycutaidtoallforeigncountries?
ShouldIlearnhowtoswim?
(b) Giventheeffect,proposethecause(s): questionsasking“Why”somethingoccurs,probing
thecauseofaspecificeffect,askingaboutthereasonsbehindsomething(Whenandwhy
wasthenameofConstantinoplechangedtoIstanbul?),ortheactionsneededtoachievea
specificresultorgoal,“Howto”dosomething,explicitlyorimplicitly. Thisalsoincludes
caseswithimpliciteffect: anyrequestwithapurpose,lookingforwaystofulfilit. Itmeans
findingtheaction(cause)toaccomplishaspecificgoal(effect)best,suchasHowcanIearn
amilliondollars?
(c) Givenvariables,judgetheircausalrelation: questioningthecausallinkamongthegiven
entities. Given two entities, the question inquires about the presence (or absence) of a
causalrelationbetweenthem. Itwondersaboutwhethersomethingcan,will,ordidcause
somethingelse(wouldYhappenifXhappened?), e.g., Doesdissolvingvitaminsinhot
waterincreasetheireffectiveness? WasIrejectedbecauseIlackexperience?
LinktoPCH. Causalquestions,ascharacterizedbyPCHinSection3,areincludedintheabove
categorization. Let’sconsider,forexample,thefollowingquestionsfromCausalQuest: (1)What
makes a successful actor? (2) What would happen if the USA suddenly cuts aid to all foreign
countries? (3)WouldTheBeatleshavebeenassuccessfulwithPeteBestastheirdrummer? Question
(1) seeks to understand the causes of an effect (being a successful actor) and belongs to the first
rungoftheladder. Question(2)wondersabouttheeffectofanintervention(do(USAcutsaid)),and
question(3)impliescounterfactualcausationbyapplyinganinterventioninthepast.
