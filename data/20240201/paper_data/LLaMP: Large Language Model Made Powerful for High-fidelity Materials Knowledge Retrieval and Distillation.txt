TheimplementationofReActforthebottom-levelagentsfortifiestherobustnessofdataretrieval.As

couldreducethenumberoftrial-and-errorsteps. Furthermore,thestoredpairscanbeusedtorefine
foundationLLMstoincreasethesuccessfulrateoffunctioncalling.
LLMs, and recent visual language models (VLMs), have demonstrated the promise to control
robotics and guide autonomous chemical experiments (Brohan et al., 2023; Boiko et al., 2023).
Inthecontextofself-drivingorautonomouslaboratory(Boikoetal.,2023;Szymanskietal.,2023),
LLMagentsmustproficientlyfetchandmanipulatedatatosupporttheirdecision-makingandoper-
ationaltasks. Forexample,accurateformationenergydatacalculatedfromfirstprinciples,coupled
withcorrectreactionenergycalculations,canaidinidentifyingviableandfasterreactionpathways.
The absence of access to a high-fidelity database through autonomous agents like LLaMP would
escalatethenumberoftrial-and-erroriterations, makingthescalingofself-drivinglaboratorypro-
hibitivelyexpensiveandpotentiallyhazardous.

7 CONCLUSION
Wepresentahierarchicalagenticframework,LLaMP,basedonRAGandReActtoextractandma-
nipulate material informatics through few-shot generalization. By grounding thoughts and actions
in high-fidelity information, LLaMP showcases the ability to integrate various modalities of ma-
terial properties and perform logical inferences to accomplish assigned tasks, all without the need
for fine-tuning. In essence, the proposed LangChain framework holds the potential to expand its
applicabilitytomultipledatasources,encompassingboththeoreticalcomputationsandexperimen-
tal data, and real-world laboratories by incorporating additional assistant agents for data retrieval
and robot control. LLaMP functions as a knowledge-aware agent, empowering users to navigate
andmanipulatecomplexmaterialsinformatics. Asnewtoolscontinuetoemerge,thereisanexcit-
ingavenueforfurtherexplorationtoascertainifthisframeworkcaneffectivelyfacilitatescientific
